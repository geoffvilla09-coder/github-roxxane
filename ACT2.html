A Filipino noodle dish with rich shrimp sauce and savory toppings

Ingredients

  • 500g rice sticks (bihon noodles)
  • 2 cups shrimp broth
  • 1/2 cup annatto powder (atsuete)
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 200g pork belly, diced
  • 1 cup shrimp, shelled and deveined
  • 1/4 cup tinapa (smoked fish) flakes
  • 4 hard-boiled eggs, sliced
  • 1 cup chicharon (pork cracklings), crushed
  • 1/2 cup green onions, chopped
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3 tbsp cooking oil
  • Calamansi or lemon wedges for serving

Instructions

  1. Prepare the sauce:
    • Mix annatto powder with 1/4 cup warm water. Strain and set aside
    • In a pan, heat oil and saut√© garlic and onions until fragrant
    • Add pork belly and cook until slightly browned
    • Stir in flour and cook for 2 minutes
    • Pour in shrimp broth and annatto water. Simmer until thickened
  2. Cook the noodles:
    • Soak rice sticks in warm water for 10 minutes. Drain
    • Blanch in boiling water for 3 minutes. Drain and set aside
  3. Assemble the dish:
    • Place noodles on a serving plate
    • Pour hot sauce over noodles
    • Arrange shrimp, tinapa flakes, egg slices, and chicharon on top
    • Sprinkle with green onions
  4. Serve immediately with calamansi wedges on the side

Preparation Time

  • Prep Time: 30 minutes
  • Cook Time: 40 minutes
  • Total Time: 1 hour 10 minutes

Chef's Tip:

For a vegetarian version, substitute shrimp broth with vegetable broth and use tofu instead of pork. Add more annatto for vibrant color!
